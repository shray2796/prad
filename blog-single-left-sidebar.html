Price Elasticity of demand

  • November 18, 2019
  • In Branding, Design
  • 3 Comments

One of our clients in the smart home devices category was launching a lower-priced device to complement its existing higher priced product. It wanted to understand the potential impact on revenue, market penetration, share shift from competitors and risk of cannibalization of the higher end SKU.We helped undertake a conjoint-based research with prospects and existing customers to understand their willingness to pay for the new product based on features and revenue optimization price points.

The below curve shows customers' willingness to pay for a product at different price points. The steeper the demand curve, the more price-sensitive customers are in relation to your product


"A good analogy is stretching a rubber band. You can stretch and stretch and even feel the tension increase in the muscles in your hands and arms as the gap from one end of the band to the other widens. But at some point you reach the limits of elasticity of the band and it snaps. The same thing happens with human systems."

John L. Casti

The below shown revenue vs price chart helps identify the revenue maximizing price of $27 for the client We used Gabor-Granger Pricing Method for the above analysis, which is a type of a randomized sequential monadic test in which respondents are given pricing options in a sequential manner to understand their preference.


We also suggested the client to launch in early Q4 since the momentum from the launch month can be taken into the Black Friday and holiday season, this strategy helped in keeping the new product the market leader for 3 consecutive months after launch.


It has become second nature for the customers to search online for best available pricing before clicking the buy button in a digital purchase. Price elasticity helps in understanding the benefit of being on a price promotion in an online word. If you are looking to establish a new price for an old product, determine new price for a new launch or evaluate the promotional price during black Friday.
